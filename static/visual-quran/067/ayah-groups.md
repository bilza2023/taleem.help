Surah Al-Mulk — Ayah Groups (1 line each)

Ayah 1 — Absolute authority and ownership is declared.

Ayah 2 — Life and death are set as a deliberate test of quality.

Ayah 3–4 — Creation is layered, inspected repeatedly, and found flawless.

Ayah 5 — The near sky is adorned and made a guarded boundary.

Ayah 6–11 — Consequence unfolds: rejection, realization, confession, regret.

Ayah 12–14 — The unseen is known; inner and outer are equally exposed.

Ayah 15 — The earth is made usable; movement and provision are allowed.

Ayah 16–17 — Apparent security is challenged from above and below.

Ayah 18 — Earlier denials are recalled as precedent.

Ayah 19–21 — Provision is shown to be controlled, withheld, and fragile.

Ayah 22 — Two paths are contrasted: upright clarity vs blind stumbling.

Ayah 23 — Human faculties are granted and ingratitude is exposed.

Ayah 24–27 — Dispersion, gathering, promise, and sudden nearness.

Ayah 28–29 — Reliance is tested when support appears threatened.

Ayah 30 — Final question: if the source is withdrawn, what replaces it?
